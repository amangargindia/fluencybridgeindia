Common Present Simple Mistakes

A structured breakdown of the most frequent errors learners make in the present simple tense, along with precise explanations and corrections.

Mistake 1 – Misusing -s/-es

One of the most common errors occurs with third person singular subjects (he, she, it). These require the verb to take -s or -es. Most verbs simply take -s (he runs, she eats), while verbs ending in -ch, -sh, -ss, -x, -o take -es (he watches, she goes).

Typical mistakes include omission (he eat) or misuse in negatives/questions (does he eats). The rule: only positive sentences take the modified verb form.

Mistake 2 – Do or Does?

Questions require auxiliary verbs. Structure: do/does + subject + base verb. Without this, sentences become structurally incomplete.

Incorrect: He like coffee?
Correct: Does he like coffee?

Mistake 3 – Double Marking

Using both does and -s together creates redundancy. The auxiliary already carries the tense.

Incorrect: Does he likes music?
Correct: Does he like music?

Verb Form after Don’t/Doesn’t

Negatives follow: subject + don’t/doesn’t + base verb. The main verb never changes form.

Incorrect: She doesn’t eats
Correct: She doesn’t eat

Present Simple vs Continuous

Present simple expresses habits; continuous expresses ongoing actions.

Incorrect: I am going to school every day
Correct: I go to school every day

Adverb of Frequency Placement

Adverbs like always, often come before the main verb but after “to be”.

Correct: She often reads, He is always late

Using “To Be” Incorrectly

Do not combine am/is/are with base verbs in present simple.

Incorrect: He is play cricket
Correct: He plays cricket

S/es after Modal Verbs

Modal verbs require base form verbs.

Incorrect: He can plays
Correct: He can play

Have vs Has

Has is used with third person singular.

Incorrect: He have a car
Correct: He has a car

Forming Questions

Correct structure: question word + do/does + subject + base verb.

Incorrect: Where you go?
Correct: Where do you go?
